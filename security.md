## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII is anything that can revel a person's identity.

### Examples
- name
- address
- email
- phone number
- bank information
- passport number
- IP address
- login details

## Indirect PII

Indirect PII is anthing that is linked to someone but could also be for someone else.

### Examples
- last name
- race
- gender
- Postcode
- job

## Authentication, Authorization and Accounting Concepts

- **Authentication** is a single piece of evidence to verify your identity.

Example:
- username and password
- pin

- **Two Factor Authentication (2FA)** is two pieces of evidence to verify your identity.

Examples:
1) Something you know: password, pin.
2) Something you have: phone, smart key
3) Somthing you are: fingerprint, facescan.

**Single Sign On (SSO)** is a form of single factor authentication that allows users to login once and access multiple apps/systems without signing in agian.

- **Autheorization** is what you are allowed to do which is determined by administartive "permissions".

**Least Privilage** is an IT principle that only grants users access to what they need to do their job.

- **Accounting** is the process of documneting users activity on a computer system.

GPS can be used for people and equitment.
**Geofencing** is a securtiy measure that enforces a virtual boundary based on real world geography.
